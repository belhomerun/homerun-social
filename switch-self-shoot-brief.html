Before you film

Compliance — read first

Never name CIMET. Do not mention CIMET, name the backend provider, or describe the tech behind the comparison. Refer only to Homerun Switch.

Safe to say

  • "Compare energy offers from our partner retailers"
  • "Could your house be paying $221 more than it needs to?" — question/conditional framing only
  • "We may earn a commission if you switch"
  • "Under 3 minutes"
  • "No sales calls. No forms. No login."
  • "I compared ours and…" — personal experience, no guarantees
  • "Made it easier", "took 3 minutes", "helped us"

Never say

  • "Save $221" — this is a claim, not a question
  • "Guaranteed savings" or any guaranteed outcome
  • "Cheapest plan", "best deal", "best price"
  • "Compare all plans" or "whole of market"
  • Any specific dollar amount you personally saved
  • CIMET, or any mention of who runs the comparison
  • "Cash back" or "cashback" — ever, for any Homerun product

$221 framing: This is an ACCC loyalty-tax stat — not a personal savings promise. Always frame as a question: "Could your house be paying $221 more a year than it needs to?" Never: "Switch and save $221."

Disclosure line for captions: "We compare a panel of partner retailers and may earn a commission if you switch." — include in every published caption.
Verbal clips

12 Hooks — film as short individual clips

Read directly to camera. 5–15 seconds each. Keep it natural — these should sound like something you'd say to a friend. Different hooks = different ad variations without re-filming.

Tip: Film each hook 2–3 times — different energy, slightly different wording. You want options in the edit.

The Reluctant Lifer
~30% of sharehouses · admin martyr · 28–42 · PRIMARY
Their enemy: Being everyone's accountant, forever.
RL 01 "If you're the one who manages every bill in your sharehouse — I did this in 3 minutes and I wish I'd done it sooner." Best with phone in hand showing switch.homerun.app.
RL 02 "69% of Aussie sharehouses haven't checked their energy plan in 12 months. I was one of them — until I actually looked." Stat sourced: 2026 survey, 3,672 sharehouses. Say it with the stat or without — both work.
RL 03 "I basically run a small business out of this sharehouse. Lease, bond, bills, the whole thing. The energy plan was the last thing I finally sorted — and it took three minutes."
The Strategic Stacker
~22% of sharehouses · deposit saver · 26–34 · PRIMARY
Their enemy: Dead money — paying $800–$1,400/month with nothing to show for it.
SS 01 "Could your house be paying $221 more a year than it needs to? That's the loyalty penalty — and it took me 3 minutes to check." $221 = ACCC stat, question framing only. Never "save $221".
SS 02 "Every dollar counts when you're saving for a place. Your energy retailer is counting on you never checking your plan. I checked."
SS 03 "I audit every bill in this house. The energy plan was the one I hadn't touched. Just compared it — takes 3 minutes, no forms, no phone calls."
Cost-of-Living
55+ · fastest-growing segment (+21% YoY) · EMERGING
Their enemy: Costs creeping up while income stays flat — everything getting harder.
CL 01 "Your energy retailer's best deals are for new customers. The longer you stay, the more they quietly charge you. That's not an accident."
CL 02 "I'd been meaning to look at the power bill for ages. Finally did — took 3 minutes, no login, no phone number, nothing."
CL 03 "No forms. No sales calls. Just your postcode. That's actually all it takes to compare energy offers for your house."
